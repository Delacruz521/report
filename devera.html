Jason Hickel

Jason Hickel is an economic anthropologist and author known for his work on global inequality and sustainability. A professor at the Autonomous University of Barcelona, he promotes the idea of de-development—urging wealthy nations to rethink progress by reducing overconsumption and supporting global fairness.

Overview

This report explores Jason Hickel’s concept of de-development, which challenges the traditional idea that constant economic growth leads to true progress. Instead of promoting more production and consumption—especially in already wealthy countries—Hickel argues that scaling down growth in rich nations can help reduce global inequality and protect the environment.

Question

How do you think the development strategies of wealthy nations affect poorer countries?

The Problem

Despite decades of development efforts, the gap between rich and poor countries is widening. A 2015 BBC report shows that even with massive investments and aid, inequality persists. Meanwhile, overconsumption in wealthier nations is harming the planet and putting society at risk. Studies reveal that 70% of people in these countries believe overconsumption is a serious issue.

Why It Matters

Human flourishing should mean more than economic success. It includes well-being, equality, and environmental sustainability. Hickel’s framework asks us to rethink progress—not as endless growth, but as creating systems that truly benefit people and the planet.

Conclusion

Hickel’s idea of de-development offers a bold but necessary alternative to traditional development. By reducing excessive growth in high-income countries, the world can move toward a fairer, more sustainable future—where all nations have the chance to flourish.
